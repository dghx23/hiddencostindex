Hidden Cost Index exposes how much of your income is silently redirected into security, load shedding, private healthcare, transport dependency and crime risk. It calculates your true disposable income and lets you compare South African cities to global ones.

Hidden Cost Index does not replace your budget. It shows what your budget is compensating for. It separates lifestyle choices from money spent to survive failing infrastructure, services and safety.

What the Hidden Cost Index measures

The model groups "system failure costs" into six categories and calculates their impact on your income, alongside an index you can compare across cities.

  • •
    Security
    Armed response, fencing, cameras, estate levies and crime-loaded insurance.
  • •
    Infrastructure failure
    Inverters, batteries, generators, backup fibre/LTE and surge damage.
  • •
    Health system gap
    Medical aid, gap cover and out-of-pocket costs because public care is not enough.
  • •
    Transport penalty
    Car instalments, fuel, maintenance and insurance due to weak public transport.
  • •
    Education uplift
    Private or semi-private schooling and extra lessons.
  • •
    Crime & risk premium
    Higher excesses, self-insurance and recurring loss from crime exposure.

The dashboard turns these into four core outputs: Hidden Cost Total, Shadow Tax Rate, True Disposable Income and a Hidden Cost Index that you can compare between Johannesburg, Cape Town and international cities.
